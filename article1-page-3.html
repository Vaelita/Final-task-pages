De-stress your environment

The first line of stress defense is to investigate how you spend your time. If you feel stressed, chances are your feelings are being triggered, at least in part, by your immediate environment. If you are inundated by distractions and noise, move and reestablish your workspace. We recommend creating a distraction-free and tranquil environment and, if possible, to work in a naturally well-lit room. Also, donâ€™t forget how your space smells! Studies suggest that aromatherapy using essential oils can help with anxiety and depression.

Remember to breathe

Breathing techniques are a quick and highly effective way to regulate our nervous system. Try to focus on breathing in for five counts and breathing out for five counts, and repeat several times. Because, remember, everything will be okay.

Alone time is important

Meditation is a universal favorite for relaxing when you work from home. It lowers stress levels, and brings calmness to your mind. Regular meditation practice helps people be able to shift their focus to the important things. It also helps to be more aware and accepting of own thoughts and feelings without judgment.
